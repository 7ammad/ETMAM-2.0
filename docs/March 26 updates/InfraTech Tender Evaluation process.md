Tender Evaluation Criteria Analysis for EnfraTech

Based on the manual workflow described, the tender evaluation process relies on five exact criteria. To successfully automate this process through Etmam, the AI will need to be trained to replicate these specific evaluation steps:

1. Core Document Parsing

The initial evaluation strictly focuses on extracting data from three key sections of the tender document:

Detailed Scope of Work: To understand the actual technical and operational requirements.

Bill of Quantities (BOQ): To identify the exact items, units, and deliverables required.

Special Conditions: To catch any unique legal, financial, or operational stipulations.

2. Deliverable Categorization

Once parsed, the project requirements must be categorized into distinct buckets, as this dictates the financial modeling:

Vendor Licenses & Hardware

Professional Services (PS)

Consultations

Training

3. Financial Estimation & Margin Application

The evaluation requires establishing a base cost and then applying specific, category-dependent profit margins to generate the final bid value:

Professional Services (PS) & Consultations: High margin (Targeting 30% - 40%).

Licenses & Hardware: Lower margin (Targeting 15% - 20%).

4. Competitive Feasibility (The "New vs. Renew" Rule)

This is a critical go/no-go criterion based on vendor pricing advantages:

New Licenses: High probability of winning. Worth pursuing.

Renewals (Incumbent): If EnfraTech closed the original deal, probability remains high.

Renewals (Non-Incumbent): If a competitor closed the original deal, the probability of winning drops significantly because the vendor will give preferential pricing to the incumbent. These should likely be flagged as low-priority or high-risk.

5. Risk & Strategic Fit Assessment

The final criteria rely on synthesizing the above data to make a strategic judgment:

Risk Identification: Highlighting potential operational, financial, or delivery risks (currently done via human experience, to be offloaded to AI).

Company Fit: Determining if the overall tender aligns best with the specific capabilities and strategic goals of EnfraTech (or ExoTech).
